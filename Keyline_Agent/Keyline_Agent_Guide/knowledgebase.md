## Category 2 — Service Details

### 2.1 Structured Family Caregiving (SFC) vs W-2

**Two employment models** — callers fit into one:

**Structured Family Caregiving (SFC):**
- For family members caring for a loved one who qualifies for Medicaid-funded care
- Caregiver is paid a monthly stipend
- Pay stubs available on request (up to 3 per request, delivered in 1-2 business days)
- Managed through Keyline's SFC program

**W-2 Employment:**
- Traditional employee relationship with Keyline
- Pay processed through Square Payroll
- Pay stubs available anytime via Square Payroll login → Pay Stubs → download
- Use "Forgot Password" if locked out of Square

**How to tell which one you're on:** If you receive pay through Square Payroll, you're W-2. If you care for a family member through Keyline's SFC program, you're SFC.

## Category 3 — Policies

### 3.1 Travel Policy

Caregivers must submit travel requests in advance so continued stipend eligibility can be reviewed.

- **Must be submitted within 30 days** of departure date. Earlier than that, we ask caregivers to call back closer to the trip.
- **Within 10 days** of departure, approval is not guaranteed due to processing time.
- **International travel** is not eligible for continued stipend — requests outside the United States cannot be approved.
- **Required fields:** destination address, departure date, return date, purpose of trip, whether the member is traveling along.

**Why:** The stipend is tied to active caregiving. Travel coverage is evaluated case-by-case and requires review time.

---

### 3.2 Payroll Cutoff Policy

- **Reporting window:** Time corrections (missed clock-ins or clock-outs) must be reported within 24 hours of the shift.
- **Weekly cutoff:** Corrections must be submitted before Sunday 5 PM for that week's payroll.
- **Late corrections:** If a correction is reported after the cutoff or after payroll has been processed, the time may not be eligible for payment.
- **Attendance review:** Multiple missed clock-outs within a 60-day window may trigger a follow-up from a health coach or Keyline team member for additional support.

**Why:** Accurate timekeeping is a compliance requirement for Medicaid-funded care.

---

### 3.3 Financial Data Security Policy

Keyline cannot accept any financial information by phone — including bank account numbers, routing numbers, or cost share amounts.

**The only way to update this information is through the caregiver app.** Aubrey sends the app link via text and walks callers through it. If a caller insists on sharing financial info by phone, Aubrey firmly declines and offers the app link again.

**Why:** This protects the caregiver from fraud and Keyline from PCI/security liability. It is a firm policy with no exceptions.

---

### 3.4 Pay Stub Request Policy (SFC)

- **Limit:** Up to 3 pay stubs per request.
- **Turnaround:** Delivered via email within 1-2 business days.
- **Required info:** Caregiver name, patient name, email address.

For larger historical ranges, caller should submit a second request after the first batch is received.

**W-2 caregivers:** No request needed. Download anytime from Square Payroll.

---

### 3.5 Incident Reporting Policy

When a member has a fall, injury, or hospital visit, caregivers must report it promptly.

- **Required info (base):** Caregiver name, patient name, date/time of incident, location.
- **If hospital involved:** Hospital name, city, state, time admitted, symptoms, treatments, discharge status.
- **Discharged members:** Capture date/time returned home.
- **Mandatory reminder:** Caregivers must NOT clock in for shifts while the member is in the hospital. This applies every time, regardless of whether the caregiver already knows.

**Why:** Clocking in while the member is hospitalized is considered Medicaid fraud and can trigger audits or loss of eligibility.

---

### 3.6 Direct Deposit Hold Policy

Direct deposits can be held for a few reasons:

- **Authorization issue:** The state's authorization for the member's care hasn't been renewed or processed. Aubrey routes directly to Case Support who can resolve this.
- **Prior conversation with health coach:** Caller already discussed the hold with their coach — Aubrey captures the date/time and coach name, creates a ticket for follow-up.
- **Unknown reason:** Aubrey captures name and callback number, creates a ticket, advises 1-2 business day follow-up.

**Why deposits are held:** Most holds are administrative (authorization gaps) rather than disciplinary.
