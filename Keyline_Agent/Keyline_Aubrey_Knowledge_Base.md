## Article 4 — Structured Family Caregiving vs W-2

Keyline has two employment models. A caregiver is on one of them.

**Structured Family Caregiving (SFC)** is for family members caring for a loved one who qualifies for Medicaid-funded home care. SFC caregivers receive a monthly stipend via direct deposit. Pay stubs are available on request (up to three per request, delivered by email within one to two business days).

**W-2 Employment** is a traditional employee relationship with Keyline. W-2 caregivers are paid through Square Payroll and can download pay stubs anytime by logging into Square Payroll and clicking Pay Stubs. A Forgot Password option is available on the login page.

**How to tell which one you're on:** If you receive pay through Square Payroll, you're W-2. If you care for a family member through Keyline's SFC program, you're SFC.

The Keyline caregiver app lets caregivers handle most routine tasks without calling in.

**What you can do in the app:**
- Submit travel requests

- View and download pay stubs (or link to Square Payroll for W-2 employees)

## Article 6 — Why Travel Has to Be Submitted in Advance

Travel requests have to be submitted within 30 days of the departure date. The Keyline stipend is tied to active caregiving, so travel coverage is evaluated case-by-case and requires review time.

- More than 30 days ahead is too early — call back closer to departure.
- 10 to 30 days ahead is the normal submission window.
- Less than 10 days ahead, approval is not guaranteed due to tight timing.
- Travel outside the United States cannot be approved for continued stipend.

## Article 7 — Why Not to Clock In During a Hospital Stay

When a member is hospitalized, the caregiver should stop clocking in for shifts until the member is discharged and care resumes at home.

This is because clocking in during a hospitalization is considered Medicaid fraud — the caregiver isn't actively providing home care during that time. Incorrect records can trigger audits or affect caregiver eligibility.

Caregivers can resume clocking in once the member is home and care has resumed.

## Article 8 — Why Financial Information Can't Be Updated by Phone

Keyline cannot accept bank account numbers, routing numbers, or cost share amounts by phone under any circumstances.

This is a firm security policy — it protects the caregiver from fraud and Keyline from PCI/security liability.

The only secure way to update direct deposit or cost share information is through the caregiver app. The agent can send the app link by text during the call.

## Article 9 — Pay Stub Request Rules

**W-2 employees** can download pay stubs anytime from Square Payroll. No request is needed.

**SFC caregivers** can request up to three pay stubs per request, delivered by email within one to two business days. For larger historical ranges, a second request can be submitted after the first batch arrives.

## Article 10 — Why a Direct Deposit Might Be Held

Direct deposits can be held for a few reasons — most are administrative, not disciplinary.

The most common reason is an authorization gap: the state's authorization for the member's care hasn't been renewed or processed yet. Case Support can resolve those directly.

If a caregiver has already spoken with their health coach about the hold, a ticket is created with the conversation details for follow-up.

If the reason is unknown, a ticket is created and someone follows up within one to two business days.

---

## Article 11 — Clock-Out Correction Rules

Time corrections (missed clock-ins or clock-outs) have rules to protect accurate records, which are a Medicaid compliance requirement.

- Corrections must be reported within 24 hours of the shift.
- They must be submitted before the weekly payroll cutoff on Sunday at 5 PM.
- Corrections reported after the cutoff or after payroll has been processed may not be eligible for payment.
- Multiple missed clock-outs within a 60-day window may trigger a follow-up from a health coach or Keyline team member for additional support.

Most correction requests are reviewed within two business days. If additional information is needed, Keyline reaches out — the caregiver does not need to call again.

## Article 12 — Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I apply to become a caregiver?**
Call 1-855-453-9546 and say you want to start services. Aubrey will collect your basic info and connect you with the Eligibility Specialist who walks you through the process. You can also visit keylinehomecare.com.

**How am I paid?**
SFC caregivers receive a monthly stipend via direct deposit. W-2 employees are paid on Square Payroll's schedule via direct deposit. Specific pay rates vary by state, hours provided, and the member's care level — the Eligibility Specialist can give exact numbers for a specific case.

**How do I reset my caregiver app password?**
Open the app, tap Forgot Password on the login screen, enter the email on file, and check your email for the reset link. For Square Payroll (W-2 employees), go to squareup.com/login and use Forgot Password.

**Does Keyline work with my insurance or Medicaid?**
Keyline's programs are primarily Medicaid-funded. Whether a specific member qualifies depends on their state, Medicaid status, and care level — the Eligibility Specialist can confirm after reviewing the member's documentation.

**Can I switch from another home care provider?**
Yes. Call 1-855-453-9546, say you want to switch providers, and you'll be connected with the Eligibility Specialist who handles the transition.

**What if my member goes into the hospital?**
Submit an incident report right away — through the app or by calling 1-855-453-9546. Do not clock in for shifts while your member is hospitalized. Resume clocking in once they're discharged and you're providing care again.

**What if I miss a clock-out?**
Report it within 24 hours of the shift and before the Sunday 5 PM payroll cutoff. Call 1-855-453-9546 and say "missed clock out." Late corrections may not be eligible for payment.

**Can I care for multiple family members?**
Generally one primary member per caregiver. Multiple-member arrangements may be possible in specific cases — the Eligibility Specialist reviews on a case-by-case basis.

**How long does enrollment take?**
Enrollment timelines vary by state and Medicaid authorization processing. The Eligibility Specialist can give a specific estimate once they review the case.

**Are you an AI? Can I talk to a real person?**
Yes, Aubrey is an AI assistant. You can reach a real person anytime — just say "I want a human" or "transfer me to someone" and you'll be connected immediately.
